✅ Cleaner by Far

22 produce items with the lowest evidence of pesticide risk in the Australian context. Conventional versions are generally a reasonable choice — saving your organic budget for the Dirty Dozen.

✅ Good news: "Cleaner by Far" does not mean pesticide-free — it means lower risk relative to other produce. More fresh produce of any kind is always better than less. These rankings are about making the best choices within that, not creating fear.
  • 1🥑 AvocadosCleanestThick inedible skin. Less than 2% of samples globally show any residues. Consistently the cleanest item on any produce list.
  • 2🍌 BananasSafePeeled before eating — flesh well protected. 2026 EWG Clean Fifteen. Note: conventional banana skins carry post-harvest fungicide residues — avoid feeding peels to wildlife.
  • 3🟣 BeetrootSafeUnderground crop, thick skin almost always peeled. Low pest pressure. Low residues internationally.
  • 4🥬 CabbageSafeOuter leaves discarded. Produces glucosinolates that naturally deter insects, reducing how much spraying is needed.
  • 5🥕 CarrotsSafeUnderground crop, skin discarded. On 2026 EWG Clean Fifteen. Low residue data in AU and globally.
  • 6⚪ CauliflowerSafeDense tightly packed head, outer leaves discarded. Low residues in all available data. Predominantly AU-grown.
  • 7🍆 EggplantSafeThick skin, lower pest pressure than thin-skinned crops. Low detection rates. Predominantly AU-grown.
  • 8🫛 Frozen garden peas (shelled)SafeProtective pod discarded before freezing. Very low residues. Distinct from snow peas — the whole pod is not eaten.
  • 9🟢 Frozen green beansSafeBlanching before freezing degrades many residues. AU-grown and processed. Significantly lower risk than fresh green beans.
  • 10🥝 KiwifruitSafeSkin not consumed. 82% of samples globally had zero detectable residues in the flesh.
  • 11🌿 LeeksSafeAllium family — outer layers discarded. Low pest pressure. Predominantly AU-grown. Consistent with onion and shallot residue profile.
  • 12🥭 MangoesSafeThick inedible skin, low residues. Buy local QLD/NT where possible — interstate transit treatment applies to some mangoes.
  • 13🍄 MushroomsSafeGrown in controlled indoor environments in AU. Low pesticide use. Generally low residues in available data.
  • 14🧅 OnionsSafePapery outer skin. Low pest pressure. Very low residues in AU data. Allium family consistently clean.
  • 15🥭 Papaya / PawpawSafeThick skin, flesh well-protected. Low detection rates. Both AU-grown and imported perform well globally.
  • 16🍍 PineapplesSafeVery thick inedible skin — near-zero residue detections globally. Mainly imported but flesh is well protected.
  • 17🎃 PumpkinSafeThick inedible skin. Low pest pressure. Flesh consistently clean globally. Predominantly AU-grown.
  • 18🍈 RockmelonSafeThick inedible rind. 61% of samples globally had zero detectable residues. Flesh well-protected.
  • 19🌱 Shallots & spring onionsSafeAllium family — papery layers, low pest pressure. Short-cycle crop, predominantly AU-grown. Low residues.
  • 20🌽 Sweet cornSafeHusk protection. Very low residues. AU sweet corn is non-GMO unlike many US equivalents.
  • 21🍠 Sweet potatoSafeUnderground crop, skin usually peeled. Low residue rates. A common AU kitchen staple with a reassuring profile.
  • 22🍉 WatermelonSafeVery thick rind. Flesh well insulated from chemicals. Low residues across all global studies consistently.

✅ General Practices

  • Check country of origin — AU-grown Dirty Dozen items are preferable to imported equivalents
  • Soak in baking soda solution (1 tsp per 2 cups water, 15 minutes) then rinse — reduces surface residues more effectively than water alone
  • Farmers markets — ask about spray practices; many small AU farms use organic methods without formal certification
  • Grow your own — focus effort on Dirty Dozen crops: strawberries, leafy greens, kale, and berries
