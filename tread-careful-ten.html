⚠️ The Tread Careful Ten

Ten commonly eaten items that don't belong on the Dirty Dozen — but carry specific concerns worth knowing about. Simple actions reduce risk significantly for most of these.

⚠ Data note: "Tread Careful" does not mean avoid — it means be informed. For most items here, simple actions (peeling, buying local, washing well) meaningfully reduce risk. Organic is advisable where the item is eaten daily or frequently by children.
  • 1 🍊 Oranges & citrus Buy AU-grown Post-harvest imazalil fungicide (linked to hormone disruption) penetrates the peel into the flesh. Found on ~90% of peeled samples. Imported citrus carries 4× higher levels than AU-grown.
  • 2 🍅 Tomatoes Buy local QLD produce is legally required to be treated with a systemic insecticide dip before interstate transit. Buy locally grown where possible and check origin labelling.
  • 3 🫚 Ginger Organic Underground tuber absorbs residual soil pesticides during growth. Almost entirely imported into Australia. Clothianidin detected at high rates in Chinese ginger. Washing is largely ineffective.
  • 4 🧄 Garlic Buy AU-grown Most supermarket garlic is imported from China. Friends of the Earth AU: China accounts for ~40% of all AQIS pesticide MRL breaches. Papery skin offers some protection — buy Australian-grown where labelled.
  • 5 🥔 Potatoes Peel well 90% of samples contain chlorpropham — a post-harvest sprout inhibitor banned in the EU since 2019, still approved in Australia. Concentrated in the skin — peel thoroughly.
  • 6 🫘 Fresh green beans Wash well Thin edible pod, high pest pressure. Acephate residues found internationally despite a decade-old ban. AU-specific data is limited but biology is comparable. Frozen green beans are lower risk.
  • 7 🫛 Snow peas & sugar snap peas Wash well Whole edible pod eaten fresh — no pesticide barrier. Predominantly AU-grown. Only industry-funded residue data available; independent testing is limited.
  • 8 🥒 Cucumbers Peel Named in AU ATDS monitoring data. Thin edible skin with moderate pesticide use. Sits in the middle of global rankings — not Dirty Dozen but not clean. Peeling removes most surface residues.
  • 9 🥒 Zucchini Wash well Thin edible skin, moderate pesticide use. Similar biology and risk profile to cucumbers. Generally AU-grown with limited independent residue data available.
  • 10 🥦 Broccoli & broccolini Wash well Lower risk than leafy greens but not negligible. Dense head provides some protection. Wash in running water getting into the florets. Organic worthwhile if eaten daily.

⚠️ Tread Careful Actions

  • Peel potatoes & cucumbers thoroughly — removes most surface residues effectively
  • Buy AU-grown garlic & citrus — significantly lower chemical load than imported equivalents
  • Buy local tomatoes — avoids the QLD mandatory interstate transit insecticide treatment
  • Organic ginger worthwhile for daily users — soil absorption makes washing largely ineffective
  • Choose frozen green beans over fresh — blanching before freezing degrades residues meaningfully
